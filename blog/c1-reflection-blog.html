What's your take on the DBC/EDA experience?

It seems to be a completely different type of learning environment than what I’d be used to. It looks to go a lot further than just learning to code.

What are your impressions?

I like the concept that it is developing you in all ways that will gear you towards becoming a good developer. I liked Shereef's analogy of kitchen or table experience. It does seem that there would be a lot more to gain from getting involved with the kitchen attitude than expecting learning to be served to you in nice polished fashion.

How do you see yourself engaging with this type of culture?

I think I will be able to engage with this culture effectively. It definitely sounds like some of it will be pushing the boundaries of my comfort zone, but I will gain a lot more from the experience by engaging in the EDA culture.

Have your expectations of EDA changed? If so, how?

My expectations have changed somewhat in that I now have a better idea of what to expect. This will be a completely different experience than any other learning I have done in the past. I already expected it to be different than other learning but couldn’t really imagine how, so at the same time my expectations were still half leaning to a more traditional learning environment.

Are you excited to participate in this kind of learning environment? Does it make you nervous?

Yes I am excited to participate in this kind of learning environment. I think a lot of aspects to this type of learning environment will be very beneficial to me. I want to get the most out of this experience and get a good start in my journey as a developer. Yes definitely nervous about it all as it will be pushing boundaries, but mostly excited.
